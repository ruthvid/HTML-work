This study explores the use of active learning and machine learning techniques to predict student anxiety, aiming to improve mental health management in educational settings. By leveraging active learning, the system identifies the most informative data points. Various machine learning algorithms, including K-Nearest Neighbours (KNN), Logistic Regression (LR), XGBoost (XGB), Naive Bayes (NB), and Random Forest (RF), are evaluated for their ability to detect anxiety patterns. The results indicate that Random Forest consistently performs well due to its robustness in handling complex data relationships.
